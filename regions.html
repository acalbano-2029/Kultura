REGIONS

Region IX - Zamboanga Peninsula

Mindanao’s history unfolds like a vibrant journey through time, beginning with early communities who shaped the island’s cultural foundations, followed by the arrival of Islam in the 13th century through Southeast Asian traders who established influential sultanates such as Maguindanao and Sulu. These kingdoms became centers of leadership, trade, and artistic tradition, standing firm against Spanish attempts at conquest and symbolizing resilience and autonomy. Alongside the Lumad groups with their distinct customs and identities, these societies contributed to the island’s rich cultural tapestry. Over time, Mindanao transformed into a dynamic region known as the “Land of Promise,” celebrated for its diverse people, traditions, and enduring spirit.
