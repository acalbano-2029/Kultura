TRAVELS

Davao City

Davao City is a bustling center of culture and nature, home to the towering Mount Apo and the Philippine Eagle Center, where the country’s rare national bird is protected. Historically inhabited by the Bagobo and other Lumad groups, the area grew from indigenous settlements into one of Mindanao’s most progressive cities. Today, it’s famous for its green spaces, vibrant festivals, and local fruits—especially the iconic, love-it-or-hate-it durian.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------




Siargao Island

Siargao Island, the country’s beloved surfing capital, is world-famous for its powerful Cloud 9 waves and scenic rock pools. Once a peaceful fishing community settled by Visayan families, the island has grown into a globally loved travel haven while keeping its laid-back atmosphere. With its coconut-lined roads, untouched beaches, and warm island culture, Siargao remains a paradise for surfers and nature lovers alike.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------




Zamboanga City

Zamboanga City is a colorful coastal destination famous for its vibrant Vinta boats, pink sand beaches, and charming blend of cultures. Once a major Spanish stronghold, the city became a melting pot of influences and the historic home of the seafaring Sama-Bajau people. Today, its unique Chavacano language, rich seafood dishes, and lively waterfront scenes make it one of the most culturally distinct cities in the Philippines.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------




Camiguin

Camiguin is a small island with big natural wonders—volcanoes, hot springs, white-sand islands, and the hauntingly beautiful Sunken Cemetery. Historically, it was home to indigenous groups and later shaped by Spanish influence, which blended with local traditions over time. Today, Camiguin’s calm atmosphere, scenic landscapes, and cultural charm make it a favorite spot for peaceful getaways.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------




Bukidnon

Bukidnon is known for its sweeping rolling hills, vast pineapple plantations, and the serene Monastery of the Transfiguration. Historically home to the Bukidnon tribes, the region preserved practices like traditional farming, rituals, and epic storytelling. Modern Bukidnon remains a nature-filled highland escape where culture, agriculture, and mountain scenery beautifully meet.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------




Tawi-Tawi

Tawi-Tawi is a remote island province known for its untouched beaches, turquoise waters, and the sea-based lifestyle of the Sama-Bajau people. Historically part of the powerful Sulu Sultanate, it became a center of trade for Muslim seafarers navigating the region’s waters. With its peaceful islands and rich cultural traditions, Tawi-Tawi offers one of the most authentic experiences in the southern Philippines.
