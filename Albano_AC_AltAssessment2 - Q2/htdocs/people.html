PEOPLE

T'boli

The T'boli of South Cotabato are known for their unique tradition of dream weaving, where artisans create the iconic T’nalak cloth based on patterns revealed in their dreams. Their culture is rich with music, intricate beadwork, brass instruments, and dances that reflect harmony with nature and the spirit world.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Manobo

As one of the largest Lumad groups, the Manobo are spread across Agusan, Bukidnon, and Davao. They are known for their epic chants called “udlipan,” elaborate traditional attire, and strong warrior heritage. Their communities maintain a deep respect for the environment, relying on farming, hunting, and forest stewardship.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Bagobo

The Bagobo of Davao are recognized for their highly detailed clothing decorated with beads, embroidery, and brass ornaments. They are skilled metalworkers and woodcarvers, and they preserve rituals connected to farming, healing, and honoring ancestral spirits. Their culture blends artistry and social structure in meaningful ways.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Subanen

The Subanen are the earliest known settlers of the Zamboanga Peninsula, traditionally living in upland and riverbank areas. They practice swidden farming and hold rituals led by balian (spiritual leaders). Music and dance play a major role in their traditions, especially during ceremonies related to harvest and healing.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Tausug

The Tausug, originally from the Sulu Archipelago, are known as the “People of the Current” because of their strong maritime heritage and skill in seafaring. They were central to the powerful Sulu Sultanate, which thrived through trade, diplomacy, and defense. Tausug culture is rich in traditional martial arts like silat, expressive dances such as pangalay, and intricate weaving and metalwork. Their society places great importance on honor, bravery, and community, making them one of the most influential cultural groups in Mindanao.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Yakan

The Yakan people of Basilan are admired for their vibrant, geometric textiles woven through complex backstrap-loom techniques. Aside from weaving, they are known for their agricultural lifestyle and musical instruments such as the kulintangan. Their cultural identity is reflected in both their clothing and their strong community traditions.
